The city of Königsberg in Prussia (now Kaliningrad, Russia) was set on both sides of the Pregel River, and included two large islands which were connected to each other, and to the two mainland portions of the city, by seven bridges. The problem was to walk through the city in a way that would cross each of those bridges once and only once.

  • reaching an island or mainland bank other than via one of the bridges, or
  • accessing any bridge without crossing to its other end are explicitly unacceptable.
